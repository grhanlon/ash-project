# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Single analyst — the portfolio manager or research analyst themselves. Using this tool alone, typically in the early morning before market open or immediately after an earnings announcement. Context: need fast, trustworthy read-through analysis without wading through sell-side notes.

## Product Purpose

Surface second-order portfolio impacts from earnings announcements. Show why a company missed, which downstream supply-chain names are exposed, and what the expected read-through is — fast, transparent, and caveat-heavy.

## Brand Personality

Sharp. Credible. Fast. No fluff, no corporate filler. The tool should feel like a high-end independent research terminal — not a consumer app, not a bank portal.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboard blue-and-white template look
- Overly playful fintech UI with gradients and illustrations
- Dense Bloomberg-terminal vomit of numbers without hierarchy
- Slow, wizard-style multi-step workflows

## Design Principles

1. **Show the answer first** — the read-through graph and ranking are the hero; inputs and settings are secondary.
2. **Never fake precision** — scores, confidence levels, and evidence quality are always visible and never hidden behind a single number.
3. **Caveats are UI, not footnotes** — data quality warnings and low-confidence links are prominent, not buried.
4. **Fast iteration** — changing a driver or adding commentary updates the downstream graph immediately.
5. **Analyst-grade density** — more information per square inch than a consumer app, but with clear visual hierarchy so it never feels cluttered.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 text contrast)
- Keyboard-navigable inputs and tables
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for any animations
- Never convey information by color alone — use icons, text labels, and position
